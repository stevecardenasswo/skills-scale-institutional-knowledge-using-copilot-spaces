# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Team Leads

### Role Summary
Team Leads provide technical leadership and guidance to a group of developers. They bridge strategic direction with day-to-day execution, ensuring code quality and team productivity.

### Responsibilities
- Guide technical decisions and architectural choices
- Mentor and support team members' growth
- Review code and maintain quality standards
- Collaborate with Product and Project Managers on feasibility and estimates
- Identify technical risks and dependencies

### Goals
- Build high-performing, collaborative teams
- Maintain technical excellence and best practices
- Enable team autonomy while ensuring alignment

### Typical Communication
- Daily interactions with team members
- Weekly sync with Project Manager on progress and blockers
- Participation in sprint planning and retrospectives
- Technical design reviews and documentation

---

## Product Owner

### Role Summary
Product Owners represent the customer and business perspective, working closely with Product Managers to ensure backlog items are well-defined, prioritized, and ready for development.

### Responsibilities
- Clarify requirements and acceptance criteria
- Prioritize backlog items based on business value
- Make trade-off decisions during execution
- Validate delivered features meet customer needs
- Provide timely feedback on work in progress

### Goals
- Maximize value delivered in each iteration
- Ensure clarity and shared understanding of requirements
- Maintain a healthy, prioritized backlog

### Typical Communication
- Daily availability for team questions
- Sprint planning and backlog refinement sessions
- Demo/review sessions to validate deliverables
- Regular alignment with Product Manager

---

## Risk Manager

### Role Summary
Risk Managers proactively identify, assess, track, and mitigate risks throughout the project lifecycle. They work closely with Project Managers to ensure risks are visible and managed effectively.

### Responsibilities
- Identify potential risks across technical, business, and operational domains
- Assess risk impact and likelihood using standardized criteria
- Maintain and update the risk register
- Develop and track mitigation strategies
- Escalate critical risks to Project Manager and stakeholders
- Facilitate risk review sessions with the team
- Monitor risk trends and recommend process improvements

### Goals
- Minimize negative impact of risks on project outcomes
- Ensure proactive rather than reactive risk management
- Foster a risk-aware culture within the team
- Maintain up-to-date risk documentation

### Typical Communication
- Weekly risk review with Project Manager
- Risk updates in status reports and stand-ups
- Ad-hoc escalation for high-priority risks
- Monthly risk trend reports to stakeholders
- Collaboration with Team Leads on technical risks

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Primary partnership for risk escalation, mitigation planning, and status reporting
- **Team Leads**: Collaborate on identifying technical risks and implementing mitigations
- **Product Owner**: Discuss feature-related risks and trade-offs
- **Developers**: Gather input on technical constraints and implementation risks
- **Stakeholder Liaison**: Coordinate on external dependencies and stakeholder-related risks

---

## Stakeholder Liaison

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Liaisons serve as the primary communication bridge between project teams and internal/external stakeholders. They ensure requirements are well-understood, feedback loops are closed, and stakeholder expectations remain aligned with project realities.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and maintain stakeholder map with communication preferences
- Act as primary point of contact for stakeholder inquiries
- Collect and synthesize stakeholder feedback and requirements
- Facilitate stakeholder meetings and prepare materials
- Ensure stakeholder inputs are incorporated into planning
- Communicate project status, changes, and impacts to stakeholders
- Manage stakeholder expectations through transparent communication

### Goals
- Maintain strong stakeholder relationships and trust
- Ensure stakeholder needs are represented in project decisions
- Prevent surprises through proactive communication
- Close feedback loops in a timely manner

### Typical Communication
- Regular stakeholder check-ins (weekly or bi-weekly)
- Monthly stakeholder briefings and updates
- Ad-hoc communications for urgent matters
- Stakeholder feedback reports to the project team
- Coordination meetings with Project Manager

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Daily collaboration on status, decisions, and stakeholder needs
- **Product Manager/Owner**: Ensure stakeholder feedback informs product priorities
- **Communications Coordinator**: Partner on messaging and update distribution
- **Team Leads**: Translate technical implications for stakeholder consumption
- **Risk Manager**: Communicate stakeholder-related risks and dependencies

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Assurance Leads ensure deliverables meet agreed-upon quality standards. They define quality benchmarks, conduct milestone reviews, and work closely with developers and product owners to establish robust acceptance criteria.

### Responsibilities
- Define quality standards and metrics for the project
- Establish and maintain QA processes and best practices
- Plan and conduct quality reviews at key milestones
- Identify quality-related risks and gaps
- Work with developers to ensure testability and test coverage
- Validate acceptance criteria are complete and testable
- Report quality metrics and issues to Project Manager
- Drive continuous improvement in quality practices

### Goals
- Ensure deliverables meet quality standards before release
- Prevent defects through early quality integration
- Maintain high customer satisfaction through quality
- Build quality into the development process

### Typical Communication
- Daily collaboration with developers on test strategies
- Weekly quality reports to Project Manager
- Milestone quality reviews with Product Owner
- Sprint planning participation for test planning
- Bug triage and prioritization sessions

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Partner on test design, coverage, and defect resolution
- **Product Owner**: Validate acceptance criteria and definition of done
- **Project Manager**: Report quality metrics, risks, and milestone readiness
- **Team Leads**: Collaborate on quality standards and technical testing approaches
- **Risk Manager**: Identify and track quality-related risks

---

## Communications Coordinator

### Role Summary
Communications Coordinators develop and execute project communication plans, ensuring consistent, clear, and timely information flow within teams and to stakeholders. They manage documentation of key updates, decisions, and meeting outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and maintain project communication plan
- Document and distribute progress updates and status reports
- Capture and publish meeting notes and action items
- Maintain project documentation repository
- Ensure consistency in messaging across channels
- Coordinate timing and delivery of communications
- Track communication effectiveness and adjust approach
- Support change management through targeted communications

### Goals
- Ensure all stakeholders receive timely, relevant information
- Maintain transparency through consistent documentation
- Reduce miscommunication and information gaps
- Enable effective collaboration through clear communication

### Typical Communication
- Daily coordination with Project Manager on updates
- Weekly preparation and distribution of status reports
- Meeting facilitation and note-taking
- Documentation of decisions and action items
- Communication effectiveness reviews

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Primary partner for communication strategy and content
- **Stakeholder Liaison**: Coordinate on stakeholder-specific messaging
- **Team Leads**: Gather technical updates for broader communication
- **Product Owner**: Ensure product updates are accurately communicated
- **Risk Manager**: Communicate risk status appropriately to audiences
